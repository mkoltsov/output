Short answer

Best inexpensive choice

A used M1 Max 64 GB or M2 Max 64 GB. Older Max bandwidth and ample memory matter more than a new base chip.

Best balanced choice

M4 Pro with 48 GB, or M5 Pro with 48 GB or 64 GB. Substantially quicker than base chips without Max pricing.

Best portable Air

M5 Air 32 GB. It is viable for occasional edits, but fanless cooling makes batches slower than the similarly configured 35.9 cm Pro.

What these times mean

Standardised workload

  • Qwen-Image-Edit-2511, one source image, approximately 1 megapixel output.
  • Quantised Q4/Q5 diffusion model and a compatible quantised text/vision encoder in current ComfyUI on PyTorch MPS.
  • Warm generation: models already loaded, no download, no cold startup, no competing heavy applications.
  • Four denoising steps with an appropriate Lightning/distillation LoRA, or 20 conventional steps.

Uncertainty

  • These are planning ranges, not measurements of 30 machines. Expect approximately ±50% depending on quant, ComfyUI build, macOS, attention path and input count.
  • Cold loading can add roughly 1–10 minutes; swapping on 16–24 GB systems can add much more.
  • Doubling pixel count commonly increases sampling time by roughly 1.7–2.5×.
  • The 41.2 cm Pro can sustain Max-chip loads slightly better than the 35.9 cm chassis; this is already absorbed into each range.

FP8 warning: the official FP8 Qwen files are not a safe assumption on Apple MPS. Current ComfyUI reports still show unsupported Float8 conversion paths. Integer GGUF or a proven Mac-specific package is the safer planning basis.

Why memory comes first

The official ComfyUI diffusion file for Qwen-Image-Edit-2511 is about 20.5 GB in FP8, while its FP8 Qwen2.5-VL encoder is about 9.38 GB, before the VAE, image tensors and application overhead. ComfyUI may offload components, so these file sizes are not a simple “add them together” RAM requirement—but they explain why 16 GB systems swap heavily and why 32–48 GB changes the experience.

  • 8 GB: exclude it for this workflow.
  • 16–18 GB: experimental Q2/Q4, reduced resolution and heavy offloading.
  • 24 GB: workable but tight at approximately 1 megapixel.
  • 32–36 GB: sensible minimum for repeated local use.
  • 48–64 GB: comfortable; lets Max/Pro chips deliver their speed without constant memory pressure.
  • 96–128 GB: useful for multiple inputs, larger graphs and keeping more models resident—not necessary for a basic single edit.
